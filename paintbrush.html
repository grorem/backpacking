Paintbrush Divide, Grand Teton NP
Class:Yuppie Hipster  +     Time: Summer 2010     Place: Grand Teton NP, Wyoming

       The Paintbrush Divide is an excellent day hike which includes a portion of the Teton Crest trail.
       Here is a map of the route going counter-clockwise. Put your mouse cursor over this image to change it into a 3D topo map.
       The first night will be spent at that first campsite with the option of maybe even spending a day extra to help acclimate ourselves to the altitude if anyone seems to be having trouble at first, as it starts out just above 9,100ft and only goes up from there with a planned max altitude of 12,500ft.
       Next, we will make our way Northeast along big sandy lake until turning North to go up and over into the cirque itself. We will then camp somewhere around here and spend a day fishing and climbing nearby mitchell peak which is at 12,500ft, yet is easily accessed by non-technical means. That's right, I said fishing. Bring poles, as the trout fishing in this area is extraordinarily great, and we will be sure to take advantage of this. When I fished in this range before, I felt like I could have tied on my multitool and still have been pulling them out all day, and fresh caught trout cooked at camp is epic tasty.
       We shall continue our way along west up and over jackass pass, which is apparently rugged enough to be just impassible for horses and mules. No problem though. We will continue on as we now turn west and eventually hike another day away and find another place to make camp.

       Continuing along in the same fashion, hiking a good bit from morning 'til afternoon and maybe taking fishing time in the afternoons. We then turn southward and eventually back to the first camp where we will camp one more night until packing it all up in the morning and start heading home. The westerly car, now turned easterly, will drop folks off at SLC intl. airport and continue on back home. That is the general plan at least, small detours to see other nearby things and fish in other lakes can and will probably be made.
If there are any questions, ask away using the facebook group or email gmrorem@gmail.com in case you don't have facebook.

Cheers,

Greg

Fitness prep: One should take this seriously as even if one is already decently fit, more never hurts, as it will only improve your enjoyment.
If one finds he or she is out of shape and no attention to improvement is given, he is likely to find himself in great discomfort and even liable to hold up the group in a trip of this caliber. The prep page offers a fitness section talking about it.
Get there: There will be a vehicle coming from the west which can pick you up in Salt Lake City at the airport if you are having to fly in for minimal travel time. For coordinating this, consult the facebook group page
